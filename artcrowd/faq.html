Artists

Getting started

What is ArtCrowd?

ArtCrowd is a crowdfunding platform dedicated exclusively to connecting Fine Artists and Fine Art Organizations with their supporters to help them realize their creative potential, build their artistic brand and fund their work. There are many options for crowdfunding out there that claim they are for artists, but none understand the particular needs of Fine Art. ArtCrowd understands art because ArtCrowd was designed by artists, art lovers and art professionals for artists, art lovers and art professionals.

What are the basics?

ArtCrowd will walk you through the steps of building a profile to identify your artistic brand, exhibit your artwork online and design a funding campaign to get results. Just follow the Step by Step Guide .

Your artistic brand is the information and art you use to build your ArtCrowd profile.

Create a funding Campaign that has a clear cut goal, such as framing a group of paintings for an exhibit, printing an exhibition catalogue or building a studio. Choose goals that are realistic and give your patrons a sense of contribution to your art.

The funding goal is the amount of money you need to raise to complete objective of the campaign, Funding is all or nothing. Your patrons will not be charged if your campaign does not reach its funding goal. So designing an attainable objective is crucial to success.

Patrons are the generous art lovers who fund your campaign to make your art goals a reality.

Rewards are the gifts, Patrons select in recognition of their funding. The best rewards are limited unique experiences and by products related to the creative process for which you are seeking funding. Rewards are not intended as a means for selling your art, as in an online gallery. Instead offer experiences like a chance to spend a day in your studio, or the chance to go on a nature walk with the artist to find sites for landscape paintings, or just meet the artist in a cafe or bar for a beverage. The most successful campaigns are the ones that do more than just raise money, they develop relationships with your supporters.

If you want to get inspired, dive into ArtCrowd and look around. You’re an artist after all, the only limits are your imagination.

How much does ArtCrowd cost?

ArtCrowd is free to create a profile, free to exhibit your art, free to build your creative brand and free to design a funding campaign. There are never any costs until your campaign is a success. ArtCrowd deducts a standard crowdfunding fee of 5% and a credit card payment processing fees (between 3% and 5%) from successfully funded campaigns and site based sales. You never have to pay out a cent to ArtCrowd. If you don’t succeed, we don’t charge you, ever!

How does ArtCrowd work?

ArtCrowd provides you with a platform to present your artistic brand. While other online platforms focus on trying to sell art, we know that is usually a race to the bottom. At ArtCrowd we want to take you to the top, so we focus on helping you to build and market your creative identity. We provide exclusive art focused crowdfunding tools you can use to attract attention to your art and find interesting ways to fund it. There are no gatekeepers like grant judges or exhibition juries. Just you and the network you build, the only limit, your ingenuity and the energy.

What kinds of art can be funded on ArtCrowd?

ArtCrowd is exclusively for original Fine Artists and Organizations working with Original Fine Art. We recognize that there are many other worthy types of creativity out there that deserve attention and funding, but we don’t want to be a “Swiss Army Knife, “ solution, trying to please everyone. We are dedicated to one thing and one thing only, finding audiences and funding Original Fine Art.

Participating on ArtCrowd

Exhibiting your artwork

Unlike many web portals, that charge artists to show their work, ArtCrowd provides a free exhibition space for anyone who builds a profile. Every effort will be made to properly credit your work and provide you with online tools to promote it, ArtCrowd does not charge agent or gallery percentages if you sell artwork displayed on ArtCrowd. What you do with your art is your business.ArtCrowd’s only interest is in helping you find funding so you can make more art.

What can I exhibit?

ArtCrowd will accept anything you can imagine finding in a fine art museum of fine art gallery. We do reserve the right to reject any work we do not feel is appropriate for ArtCrowd, but in most cases, we welcome all kinds of original fine art from all over the world. There is no qualification process or jury selection. ArtCrowd is not a place for reproductions, copies of forgeries. If you make original fine art, we want you to become part of the ArtCrowd.

The one thing all successfully run crowdfunding campaigns share is networking. If you thought the work was done when you finalized your campaign and took it live, you are in for a surprise. Like a tree falling in a forest, no one will know about your campaign if they don’t know about it... and get reminded. A campaign launched without on-going development , regularly posted updates and even new rewards, will most likely fall short. Share your new content on social media, get your friends to share it too. ArtCrowd is designed to provide you with lots of ways to share your ArtCrowd activity. So get the word out. And respond too. Your future patrons have questions. Be prepared to answer them as soon as possible. Rapid response rate is a key predictor of funding follow through. The longer you take to reply, the more likely your supporters will have moved on.

ArtCrowd rewards successful campaigns that have Made It. Creating and running a successful campaign is a lot of work. As you push past your finish line goal, you’ll understand why we say you “made it.” Your success is our success and we are proud of your accomplishment so we want the world to know. Your successful campaigns and the art in your gallery will become a part of our Permanent Collection in the ArtCrowd Museum. You will be able to continue to offer selected rewards in our Museum Store. .

Funding Art

Campaigns offer Patrons the opportunity to select from a number of gift Rewards in exchange for funding from the Campaign they are supporting. Alternately, a Patron can choose to not receive a reward for their funding. But either way, patronage ushers an art lover into the world of the artist they are funding with a unique view into their process and a chance to be personally involved in its creation.Managing your funding

Receiving Rewards

Within about 15 days of a Campaigns successful completion, ArtCrowd will fund the campaign creator. The campaign creator is solely responsible for fulfilling the promised rewards according to the promised schedule for each reward. It is the obligation of every campaign creator to see to it that their patrons receive the rewards they expect. ArtCrowd offers creators fulfillment management tools online to monitor the process but does not intervene in an individual campaigns execution or stratification of delivery.

Patrons need to appreciate that they are supporting the creation of something new, not ordering an artwork that already exists. Problems may arise that prevent a creator from fulfilling a reward promise but then the creator must make every reasonable effort to find a way of bringing the project to a satisfying conclusion for their backers. For more information, see our Terms of Use.

Creating a Member Art Collection

Every Creator on ArtCrowd can exhibit an Online Gallery of their artwork at no cost. Each artwork features a diamond icon during hover, that when selected allows any ArtCrowd member viewing the artwork to add it to their own personal Art Collection. Member Art Collections function differently from Creator Online Galleries. The Online Galleries feature work by the creator that is uploaded to a their profile allowing for captioning of artwork credits and incorporation into funding campaigns, while Art Collections are available to ArtCrowd members only for art appreciation and social sharing. Artworks in a Collection cannot be caption edited by the collector and the creator’s ownership of the art is protected. Patrons are encouraged to add artworks they appreciate to their Art Collections in their profiles and spread the word about the art they love by social sharing. Just as it is free to exhibit in the ArtCrowd, it is also free to create your own ArtCrowd Art Collection.

Troubleshooting

How do I delete my account?

Your account can be deleted from your account page. Warning: this is not reversible!

Log In Problems

I forgot my password, how do I log in?
Click the 'Forgot?' link on the login page to request a password reset. Just input the email address associated with your account and we'll send you an email with a link to reset your password.

My patronage was declined. What do I do?

If your funding charge was declined by the credit card processing, log in to ArtCrowd and click the “ Fix payment ” button. This will guide you through the process of changing your payment method. We'll also send you an email within 48 hours with a direct link to correct the issue. But don't delay: patrons have 7 days to resolve payment issues before they're dropped from the project. We'll also automatically retry your pledge at the end of this timeframe.

It may not necessarily be the case that there’s a specific issue with your card, so much as that you’ll need to ask your bank why this charge in particular is being declined. You may also want to include the details of this transaction, such as:

- The charge being from ArtCrowd.
- The amount pledged for the project.
- The date when ArtCrowd attempted to collect your pledge
- this was the date that the project you backed ended. If the project hasn't ended yet, you can use the date you attempted to pledge.

Once you’ve contacted your bank, feel free to log in to the ArtCrowd account from which you pledged and click the “Fix Payment” button in the “Action Needed” banner.

Why is my pledge being authorized?

When you fund a campaign, our payments provider may temporarily authorize your card— this is just to make sure that your card works and can be successfully charged when the time comes. Occasionally, multiple preauthorizations may occur per pledge. (Since funding on ArtCrowd is all-or-nothing, you'll only be charged if the project is successfully funded.)

How come my Facebook account is showing up as somebody else?

If you are using a computer that is logged in to someone else's Facebook, connecting your ArtCrowd account to Facebook will create a link to that other person's account. Make sure that whichever computer you're working on is either logged out of Facebook or already logged in as yourself.

Why are Campaigns Time Limited All-or-Nothing?

While it might seem like a good idea to make your campaign open ended and not have an all-or-nothing goal, the truth is that crowdfunding platforms that work with deadlines and goals significantly outperform those that don’t. Your friends and supporters mean well, but without time limits and a focused goal. they will procrastinate and forget. It’s human nature. If you’re worried that you might not get your funding, start by designing campaigns with modest attainable goals. There is no limit on the number of campaigns you can run, but we only allow you to do one at a time. Trust us, you don’t want to multitask task a funding campaign. Worried about falling short creating a public campaign? We’ve got you covered, try ArtCrowd’s exclusive Vernissage.

What is Vernissage?

Unlike other all-or-nothing crowdfunding sites, we understand that running a campaign is a learning process and what seems like the right mix of ideas and incentives at the start can prove otherwise when you get audience reaction. That’s where ArtCrowd’s exclusive Vernissage comes in. Vernissage is based on the traditional invitation only preview exhibits famous galleries offer their most valued patrons. ArtCrowd Vernissage lets you run a full fledged trial campaign you can tweak and refine, test rewards, the scope and even the goal of the campaign. Only supporters you invite can see the Vernissage and receive it’s rewards. You can even offer exclusive incentives for Vernissage Patrons. Then when you have fine tuned your campaign, switch to live mode to let the whole world get involved.

What Guarantees do Patrons have?

Like any investment, Patrons should show due diligence in selecting the artists they support through ArtCrowd. Investing in any speculative area is inherently risky. ArtCrowd offers creators fulfillment management tools online to monitor the process but does not intervene in an individual campaigns execution or stratification of delivery.

Patrons need to appreciate that they are supporting the creation of something new, not ordering an artwork that already exists. Problems may arise that prevent a creator from fulfilling a reward promise but then the creator must make every reasonable effort to find a way of bringing the project to a satisfying conclusion for their backers. For more information, see our Terms of Use .

Can ArtCrowd issue refunds?

Once a campaign is successfully funded, completed and your credit card has been charged, ArtCrowd cannot issue a refund to the Patron. Transactions are between backers and creators directly. Creators receive all funds (less fees) soon after their campaign ends, ArtCrowd is just a processing agent for the transactions. However Patrons can change or cancel their funding up until 48 hours before the end of the campaign.

Does ArtCrowd offer Guarantees for Rewards?

ArtCrowd was created to help artists fund their creative needs for their art. ArtCrowd is not fiscally structured to offer guarantees the way a risk adverse profit focused traditional operation like a gallery or agent can (who take between 20-60% of an artists earnings), or like a pay to play service that charges artists huge fees up front to get their art seen. ArtCrowd charges only a slight fee on successful campaigns so we can encourage new art. With anything new, one has to accept a bit of risk. We think it is worth it to encourage new art and see the options for creating art grow.

What Protections does ArtCrowd offer?

There are no charges on Artcrowd for the Patron or against the creator’s funding until and unless a campaign has been successful. ArtCrowd will never ask you for money other than to follow up to collect funds you, yourself have promised a successful campaign, ArtCrowd does its best to monitor suspicious activity and follow up on any concerns reported by its users. Anyone found to be engaged in unethical activity will be warned if minor, and banned if serious. Hate speech, intolerance and threats will not be tolerated. ArtCrowd does not ban nudity, which is an important form of artistic expression, but it will not permit pornography.
